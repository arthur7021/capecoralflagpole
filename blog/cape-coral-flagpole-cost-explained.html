Cape Coral Flagpole Cost Explained: Where the $420,000 Actually Went

Published July 5, 2026

Cape Coral's new 250-foot flagpole at Bernice Braden Park is officially the tallest flagpole in Florida — and it came with a price tag that's been the center of local debate since the project was approved. Here's the full breakdown.

The Total: About $420,000

The project cost roughly $420,000 all in. Of that, about $330,000 came from private donations raised through the city's "Raise the Flag" fundraiser, and about $90,000 came from taxpayer/city funds.

Ongoing Costs Don't Stop There

Maintenance on a 250-foot pole isn't cheap: the city estimates roughly $16,000 per year to keep it running. And because of its size, a proper flag for the pole costs around $50,000 each — a recurring expense every time one needs replacing.

Why the Delay Mattered

Construction ran into delays before the July 4th ceremony, and the contract reportedly included a $1,294-per-day penalty clause for missing the deadline — which put extra pressure on the timeline heading into the holiday.

Was It Worth It?

Three city council members voted against the project, and reaction in Cape Coral has been mixed since the flag went up. Some residents see it as a proud civic landmark; others see it as an avoidable taxpayer expense for a symbolic structure.
