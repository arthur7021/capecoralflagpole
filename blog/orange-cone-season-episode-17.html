Why Cape Coral feels like one big construction zone right now

Cape Coral has been in near-constant road and utility work for years — widening projects, stormwater upgrades, and the city's long-running canal and pipe replacement program all compete for the same intersections at the same time. Add in routine repaving and private development driveways, and it's easy for a resident to hit three separate detours just crossing town.

Unlike a lot of Florida cities with a grid laid out for easy alternate routes, Cape Coral's canal-heavy layout means there often isn't a good detour — bridges and canal crossings are chokepoints, so closing even one lane near a bridge or a major parkway can back up traffic for blocks. That's exactly the kind of bottleneck Burrowing Al keeps running into near the flagpole.

Burrowing Al's official theory: the cones are reproducing. His investigation continues.
