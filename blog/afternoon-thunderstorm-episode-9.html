The Afternoon Thunderstorm

Published July 9, 2026

Classic Florida afternoon: sunny bridge, iced coffee in hand — then one single dark cloud parks directly over Burrowing Al and pours rain on him alone while everyone twenty feet away stays bone dry. Tourists panic and scramble for cover; the local angler doesn't even look up. Twenty minutes later the sun's back, the pavement's steaming, and Al's wringing out his cap like it never happened.
