📅 The Flagpole Timeline

From approval to raising — here's how this project unfolded.

📋
March 2026
City Council Approves the Contract
Cape Coral approves a contract for a 250-foot flagpole at Bernice Park with a price tag of up to $420,000. A fundraiser is launched to cover costs.
💰
Spring 2026
Fundraising Raises $281K+
The city raises over $281,000 in donations — but the project cost exceeds that, leaving taxpayers on the hook for the gap plus all future maintenance.
🏗️
June 4, 2026
Substantial Completion Deadline — Missed?
The contract required "substantial completion" by June 4. Social media posts claimed the contractor missed this deadline and should be fined $1,294/day. The city disputed the framing.
⚠️
June 18, 2026
City Pushes Back on Delay Claims
"The final completion date for this project is July 3rd," said Cape Coral's communications manager Melissa Mickey. The city says no fines apply unless the project isn't finished by July 3.
🎉
July 4, 2026 · 2:00 PM
🚩 The Flag Goes Up — "Raise the Flag" Ceremony
The official "Raise the Flag" ceremony at Bernice Braden Park, 2051 Cape Coral Pkwy. Open to the public. America's 250th birthday. Florida's tallest flagpole officially raised.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How tall is the Cape Coral flagpole?

250 feet — making it the tallest flagpole in the state of Florida, at Bernice Braden Park.

Where is the Cape Coral flagpole located?

Bernice Braden Park, 2051 Cape Coral Pkwy E, Cape Coral, FL.

How much did the Cape Coral flagpole cost?

About $420,000 total — roughly $330,000 from private donations and about $90,000 from taxpayer/city funds.

Who paid for the Cape Coral flagpole — donations or taxpayers?

Mostly private donations (~$330K), with the remaining ~$90K coming from city/taxpayer funds. See the full breakdown above.

How much does it cost to maintain the flagpole each year?

Roughly $16,000 per year in ongoing maintenance, plus about $50,000 every time the giant flag itself needs replacing.

Is the Cape Coral flagpole taller than the Washington Monument or the Statue of Liberty?

No. The Washington Monument is 555 ft and the Statue of Liberty is about 305 ft (base to torch) — both taller. At 250 ft, Cape Coral's flagpole isn't the tallest structure around, but it is the tallest flagpole in Florida.

When is the Cape Coral flagpole raising ceremony?

July 4, 2026 at 2:00 PM at Bernice Braden Park.

Why is the Cape Coral flagpole controversial?

The debate centers on whether the roughly $90,000 in taxpayer funding was a good use of city money versus what else that could have paid for — see our breakdown of alternatives above, and vote in our community poll.

What happens if the flagpole construction is delayed?

The contractor faces $1,294 per day in liquidated damages for missing the completion deadline.
