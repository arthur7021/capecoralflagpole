Overview

The Cape Coral flagpole is a 250-foot flagpole erected at Bernice Braden Park in Cape Coral, Florida, making it the tallest flagpole in the state. Approved by the Cape Coral City Council in March 2026 and tied to the timing of America's 250th anniversary, the project carries a total cost of roughly $420,000, funded through a mix of private donations and city (taxpayer) money. The flagpole became a local flashpoint over how that funding split, plus ongoing maintenance costs, should be weighed against other municipal priorities.

The official "Raise the Flag" ceremony is scheduled for July 4, 2026 at 2:00 PM, open to the public, at 2051 Cape Coral Pkwy E.

History & Approval

In March 2026, the Cape Coral City Council approved a contract for a 250-foot flagpole at Bernice Braden Park, with a project price tag of up to $420,000. The timing was framed around America's 250th anniversary on July 4, 2026. A public fundraiser, "Raise the Flag," was launched by the city to help cover the cost, inviting residents and businesses to donate.

Funding & Cost Breakdown

The project's total construction cost is reported between $420,000 and $450,000 depending on the source. Roughly $330,000 was raised through private donations via the city's fundraiser, leaving an estimated $90,000 gap covered by taxpayer/city funds. Timeline

  1. March 2026 — City Council approves the flagpole contract; fundraiser launched.
  2. Spring 2026 — Fundraiser reaches $281,000+ in donations, short of full project cost.
  3. June 4, 2026 — Contract's "substantial completion" deadline. Social media posts alleged the contractor missed it and should face daily fines.
  4. June 18, 2026 — City pushes back publicly: communications manager Melissa Mickey states the actual final completion date is July 3, and no fines apply before then.
  5. July 4, 2026, 2:00 PM — "Raise the Flag" ceremony at Bernice Braden Park; flagpole officially raised, coinciding with America's 250th birthday.

Controversy & Criticism

Criticism has centered on three points: the roughly $90,000 taxpayer contribution toward construction, the ~$16,000/year ongoing maintenance bill, and the ~$50,000 cost of each flag replacement — all recurring costs borne by the city after the one-time donation-funded portion is spent. Supporters point to the symbolic timing with the nation's 250th anniversary and note the majority of construction was donor-funded. Separately, a dispute arose over whether the contractor missed its June 4 completion deadline; the city maintains the binding deadline was July 3 and that liquidated damages of $1,294/day apply only after that date.

Height & Cost Comparisons

Height

At 250 feet, the Cape Coral flagpole is the tallest flagpole in Florida, but it is shorter than several famous American landmarks: the Washington Monument (555 ft) and the Statue of Liberty (~305 ft) are both taller.

What else $420,000 could fund locally

  • ~840 pothole repairs at roughly $500 each
  • 4 new neighborhood parks (typical small park install: $80K–$120K)
  • 3+ police officer salaries for a year (~$120K/yr with benefits in SW Florida)
  • Hurricane-prep storm drainage improvements
  • Water/sewer utility upgrades
  • School supplies for 2,000+ students at ~$200/student

FAQ
